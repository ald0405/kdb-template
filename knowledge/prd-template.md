# PRD template

KDB follows this structure when drafting a PRD. Fill in your team's conventions and adjust section headings to match how you work.

---

## [Feature name] — PRD

**Status:** Draft / In review / Approved  
**PM:** YOUR_NAME  
**Last updated:** YYYY-MM-DD  
**Jira epic:** YOUR_EPIC_LINK  
**Notion:** YOUR_NOTION_PRD_URL  

---

### Problem

One paragraph. What's broken or missing, who feels it, and how do we know?

- User signal: (quote, research finding, support ticket trend)
- Scale: how many users / how often?
- Why now?

---

### Goals

| Goal | Metric | Target | Baseline |
|------|--------|--------|----------|
| Primary | e.g. activation rate | +X pp | XX% |
| Secondary | e.g. D7 retention | +X pp | XX% |
| Guardrail | e.g. support contacts | no increase | XX/wk |

---

### Non-goals

What this PRD explicitly does not cover. Prevents scope creep.

- We are not solving X
- We are not changing Y

---

### Solution

Brief description of the approach. Defer detail to designs and tech spec.

**User journey (happy path):**

1. Step one
2. Step two
3. Step three

**Edge cases to handle:**

- Edge case A → behaviour
- Edge case B → behaviour
